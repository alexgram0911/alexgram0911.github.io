ELDRIC FENBLIGHT

Eldric Fenblight was an agent of the Inquisition, sent to purge what they called blight—strange growths, unnatural changes, entire villages that didn’t fit their idea of order. He was good at it. Efficient. Unquestioning. If a place was marked, Eldric burned it out. No hesitation.

Until one mission… felt wrong.

The land wasn’t dying. It was changing. What the Inquisition called rot… wasn’t corruption. It was the world correcting itself—rebalancing after something deeper had broken.

For the first time, Eldric didn’t act. And in that moment, everything cracked.

He saw it clearly: The Inquisition didn’t destroy evil. They destroyed anything they didn’t understand.

So Eldric walked away. No orders. No explanation. Just gone.

They hunted him for it. He didn’t make it far. They found him. Former allies. Former brothers. He didn’t fight them all. And when it was over… Eldric Fenblight was dead.

But something didn’t let that be the end. The land. The rot. The balance he had finally understood. It brought him back. Not as he was. As something… meant to finish the work properly.

Eldric walks the world as an Oath of Pestilence Paladin, but not for the Inquisition, and not for any god.

He destroys what is truly corrupted.

He protects what is naturally changing.

He no longer mistakes growth for disease.

The Inquisition calls him a traitor. The innocent don’t know what to call him. And Eldric… Doesn’t care what he’s called. Only that this time—He gets it right.
