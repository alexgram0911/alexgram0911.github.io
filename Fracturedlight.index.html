Fractured Light

A grim echo of Lanternlight. You are what remains after the road stops forgiving. Every roll moves you forward. Most outcomes leave a mark.

Roll: d20 + stat LIGHT: 6–8 (per player or shared) Tone: escalation & no return
Lanternlight Rules Character Sheet

Your Character

You are a traveler carrying a familiar role in an unfamiliar place. The same instincts that kept you safe on the Lanternlit Trail still exist. Here, they cost more.

  • Role (Engineer, Medic, Scout, Watcher, etc.)
  • Something you’re good at
  • Something you’re afraid of

Echo: These are the same anchors as Lanternlight. In Fractured Light, fear is not flavor. It is leverage.

Your Stats

Use the same four stats as Lanternlight (or your table’s equivalents):

Trail
Navigation, movement, safe routes.
Care
Tending, repair, keeping others steady.
Clever
Noticing, problem-solving, deception.
Endure
Fear, cold, pain, refusing to stop.

Assign +2, +1, 0, and −1 among your stats.

LIGHT

LIGHT measures what you have left: breath, stability, self. It is not only injury. It is the distance between you and collapse.

Starting LIGHT
6–8 (per player) or a shared pool if you want tighter tension.
When LIGHT drops
Bad rolls, danger, helping others, harsh environments, final sequences.
At 0 LIGHT: you do not “go unconscious.” You break. Choose what that looks like: freeze, panic, rage, shutdown, surrender. You remain present, but unreliable.

Rolling the Dice

When something is uncertain or risky, the GM calls for a roll:

d20 + stat vs DR (Difficulty Rating)

Typical DR: 12–16 Hard spikes: 16

Success (≥ DR)

You do what you intended. Clean. Controlled.

Fractured Success (within 2 below DR)

You succeed… but choose or suffer one cost:

  • Lose 1 LIGHT
  • Gain a MARK
  • Advance +1 TURN

Failure (3+ below DR)

You fail and the world presses in. The GM applies consequences:

  • Lose 1–2 LIGHT
  • Gain a MARK
  • Advance +1 TURN (often mandatory)

Natural 20s and Natural 1s

  • Natural 20: a brief mercy. reduce a cost, avoid a mark, or hold the TURN.
  • Natural 1: escalation. the GM adds an extra cost or advances the TURN.

Helping Each Other

You may help another character if it makes sense in the story.

  • Helping costs 1 LIGHT
  • Helping gives advantage or improved positioning

Echo: In Lanternlight, helping costs Lanternlight. Here, it costs what that Lanternlight was protecting.

MARKS

MARKS are permanent complications. They do not fade when the scene ends. They are what the journey leaves behind.

Examples
  • Cracked Visor (impaired sight)
  • Shaking Hands (harder to assist)
  • Static in the Head (SNOW-E bleeds through)
  • Noticed Below (something tracks you)
How to use
  • Trigger when dramatically appropriate
  • Offer hard choices tied to the mark
  • Let marks change plans, not just numbers

The TURN Track (0 → 6)

The Evening Turn is escalation made visible. When the TURN advances, the world becomes less patient.

Advance TURN when
  • Rolls fail
  • Time is wasted
  • Noise/chaos escalates
  • Combat drags on
TURN 2
The world shifts.

Terrain destabilizes. Small things go wrong.

TURN 4
You are not alone.

Threats escalate. Marks bite harder.

TURN 6
The Turn completes.

Something arrives. Routes collapse. Final sequences accelerate. You can still succeed. It will not be clean.

A Final Note

Fractured Light is not about winning fights. It is about what you are willing to spend to keep the beacon lit.

Some actions always succeed.
They just cost everything.
