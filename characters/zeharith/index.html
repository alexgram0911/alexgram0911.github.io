ZEHARITH
The Forgotten Descent

Before the Battle of Gosehr Fields, before the purge of the Temple Moriatha, Zeharith was something else. He doesn't remember what. It comes in fragments: weightlessness, a voice like low thunder, a feeling of purpose. These fragments visit him like fever dreams after long stretches in the Mistways, and he has learned to drink until they stop.

He has been alive for at least 860 years. Before that, he existed for untold ages more — a celestial in the service of Moriatha. When Empress Radyja slaughtered her kin and purged the Temple, every celestial bound to the fallen god was ripped from the heavens and made flesh.

Zeharith became a Downcast: grey-skinned, black-eyed, carrying the fading echo of a dead god's power in his blood.

800 mortal years wandering the Mistways has buried that identity completely. Somewhere along the way, the angel forgot he had ever been an angel at all. His most recent life took shape in Otteriel. He surfaced through the Fading Pass decades ago, drawn back for reasons he couldn't articulate.

In Eiermoss, the Prelate Asier took him in as a useful curiosity. He studied at the Ecole de Fée under Frederick Fawst, who saw in him an unusual gift for the magic of decay — the inverted echo of a healer's instinct turned inside out by centuries of forgetting.

He specialized as a Plague Doctor, serving the Ulmist Inquisition as a compounder of discreet solutions. He told himself he was surviving.

Then the Mistways closed. The Fading Pass sealed. And for the first time in almost 900 years, Zeharith is trapped in the one place that should mean the most to him — if only he could remember why.
